What is time-sharing?

Time-sharing systems were designed

  • to allow multiple users to connect to a powerful mainframe computer simultaneously
  • to interact with it via a console that ran their applications by sharing processor time.

What is TCP/IP?

  • The TCP on the sender’s machine breaks the message into packets and send them out.
  • The IP is instead the part of the protocol concerned with “the addressing and forwarding” of those individual packets.

1983 ARPANET and MILNET

    The ARPANET was effectively divided in two distinct networks:
  • ARPANET, mainly dedicated to research
  • MILNET, a military operational network, protected by strong security measures like encryption and restricted access control.

  • 1975 - end of ARPANET as a research project.
  • 1979-1983 - ARPANET switched from Network Control Program to TCP/IP.
  • 1983 - ARPANET split into two parts APANET and MILNET.
  • 1990 - ARPANET officially closed.
